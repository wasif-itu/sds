**Project Title: Spatial Disparities Between Scenic Potential and Accessibility in Northern Pakistan**

**Problem Statement**

Northern Pakistan, particularly Gilgit-Baltistan and upper Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, contains globally significant mountain landscapes but exhibits uneven tourism development. Infrastructure is concentrated along major corridors such as the Karakoram Highway, while potentially high-scenic regions remain relatively remote and underdeveloped. Despite the economic importance of tourism, there is no quantitative, spatially explicit framework that systematically identifies disparities between scenic potential and road-based accessibility at the sub-district (Tehsil) scale.

The central research questions are:
(1) Where are statistically significant local clusters and spatial outliers of scenic potential located?
(2) Which Tehsils represent “High Scenic–Low Accessibility” regions?
(3) Does accessibility meaningfully explain variation in scenic potential across the region?

Addressing these questions is significant for sustainable tourism planning, conservation prioritization, and balanced regional development policy.

---

**Methodology**

The study will analyze 60–80 Tehsils across Gilgit-Baltistan and upper Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. Two composite indices will be constructed:

1. **Scenic Potential Index (SPI)**
   SPI will combine standardized environmental indicators:

   * Terrain Ruggedness Index (derived from SRTM 30m DEM following Shawn J Riley et al., 1999)
   * Forest cover (%)
   * Water bodies (%)
   * Snow/ice coverage (%)

   Land-cover data will be sourced from ESA WorldCover 2021. All components will be normalized and combined using weighted aggregation, with sensitivity analysis conducted to test robustness under alternative weight structures.

2. **Accessibility Index (AI)**
   AI will integrate:

   * Road density (primary + secondary roads per km²)
   * Euclidean distance from Tehsil centroid to nearest major road

   Road data will be extracted from OpenStreetMap via OSMnx.

Spatial analysis will include:

* Global Moran’s I to assess overall spatial autocorrelation (following Luc Anselin, 1995).
* Local Indicators of Spatial Association (LISA) to identify clusters and spatial outliers.
* Ordinary Least Squares regression (SPI ~ AI) to evaluate spatial association between accessibility and scenic potential.
* Conditional spatial lag modeling if residual spatial dependence is detected.

All analyses will be conducted using Python and open-source geospatial libraries (geopandas, rasterio, libpysal, esda).

---

**Objectives**

1. Develop a reproducible, open-source framework for measuring scenic potential and accessibility.
2. Identify statistically significant high-scenic / low-accessibility Tehsils.
3. Quantify the spatial relationship between accessibility and scenic potential.
4. Provide evidence-based guidance for sustainable tourism and conservation planning.

---

**Expected Outcomes**

The project will generate:

* Tehsil-level SPI and AI datasets.
* Maps of scenic hotspots, accessibility gaps, and LISA cluster classifications.
* A regression model quantifying accessibility–scenic association.
* Identification of priority regions warranting further feasibility assessment.

The study contributes a transparent, data-driven method for spatial planning in mountain regions and provides a replicable framework applicable beyond Northern Pakistan.
