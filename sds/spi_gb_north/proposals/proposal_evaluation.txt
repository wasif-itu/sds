## ⚠ Critical Conceptual Issue

An R² of **0.85** with:

* n = 12
* Composite index vs composite index
* Both standardized

is almost certainly inflated.

Why?

Because:

* SPI and AI are both constructed indices
* Both use z-score normalization
* Both spatially structured

This introduces mechanical correlation risk.

A reviewer might ask:

> “Is this a statistical relationship or an artifact of index construction?”

You need to explicitly add:

> “Given small pilot sample and index-based construction, R² may be inflated. Full-sample regression will provide more reliable effect estimates.”

This preempts skepticism.

---

## ⚠ Causality Section — Good But Overreaching

You mention:

* Temporal precedence
* Instrumental variables
* Road construction dates

That’s intellectually impressive.

But:

You are not actually proposing a feasible IV strategy yet.

Be careful not to imply you will conduct causal inference unless you truly can.

Safer framing:

> “This study evaluates spatial association. Causal mechanisms are hypothesized and proposed for future research.”

That keeps scope realistic.

---

## ⚠ Spatial Lag Specification Issue

You wrote:

> W*SPI = median scenic potential of neighboring Tehsils.

That is **not standard spatial lag specification**.

Standard spatial lag model:

[
SPI = ρ W SPI + β AI + ε
]

Where W*SPI is the spatially lagged dependent variable (weighted average), not median.

You should not redefine it as median.

Use row-standardized weights → spatially weighted mean.

This is a technical correction you must make.

## One Conceptual Clarification Needed

You state:
